And honestly? I think that's the sweet spot. Use it as your research assistant, not your travel guru. Ask it for frameworks, not final answers. "Give me three neighborhood options in Lisbon for families" - great. "Tell me the exact opening hours of this specific attraction" - verify that yourself.

If you're thinking about trying it for your next trip, here's what I'd suggest. Be specific. The more context you give, the better. Tell it about your travel style, your budget, who you're traveling with. And treat the first output as a rough draft, not a final plan. Ask follow-up questions. Challenge it. "Is there a quieter neighborhood than this?" "What's a local restaurant off the tourist path?"
